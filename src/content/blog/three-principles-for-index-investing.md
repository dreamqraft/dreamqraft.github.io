*This is a personal note, not investment advice. Translated from the Korean post ["지수 추종 장기 투자를 위한 세 가지 원칙"](../../investment/index-fund-principles/).*

Market predictions fail again and again, but principles protect your behavior when the market shakes. Here are the three I try to keep.

## 1. Look at costs first

You cannot control returns, but you can control costs. I pick products based on the real total cost — expense ratio (TER), trading fees, and taxes combined.

## 2. Rebalance by the calendar

If you time your rebalancing based on market conditions, it eventually becomes market prediction. Instead, I mechanically restore target weights on fixed dates (e.g., every January and July).

| Asset class | Target weight |
|-------------|---------------|
| Equities (domestic + global) | 70% |
| Bonds | 20% |
| Cash equivalents | 10% |

## 3. Keep a written record

If you write down the reason for every buy and sell at the moment you make it, you can later check whether you keep repeating the same mistakes. The investment category of this blog is that record.
